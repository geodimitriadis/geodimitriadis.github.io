I am an observational astrophysicist specialising in transient astronomy, with a particular focus on <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jlqnKu82UxU" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"> Type Ia supernovae</a>, their progenitor systems, and the diversity of thermonuclear transients.

I completed my PhD in Astronomy at the <a href="https://www.southampton.ac.uk/research/institutes-centres/astronomy-group" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"> University of Southampton</a>, following an MSc in Physics and Astronomy at the <a href="https://api.uva.nl/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"> Anton Pannekoek Institute for Astronomy</a> in Amsterdam and a Bachelor’s degree in Physics at <a href="https://www.physics.auth.gr/en/home/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"> Aristotle University of Thessaloniki</a>.  

My research career has taken me through positions at the <a href="https://transients.ucsc.edu/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"> University of California in Santa Cruz</a>, <a href="https://www.tcd.ie/physics/research/groups/explosive-transients/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"> Trinity College Dublin</a>, and <a href="https://www.lancaster.ac.uk/physics/research/astrophysics/observational-astrophysics/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"> Lancaster University</a>, where I am currently a Marie Skłodowska-Curie Fellow.

My work combines large-scale transient surveys with detailed optical and near-infrared spectroscopy, with the aim of understanding the explosion physics and improving the use of Type Ia supernovae as cosmological tools.
